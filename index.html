Rego: Rescue on the go

Rego is a digital service that aims to tackle the ongoing issue of food waste in Singapore, through the selling of surplus food at discounted prices. Making the rescue of food convenient and attractive, Rego aims to instil the value of food sustainability as important and chic.

Digital solution to beat food surplus

In 2018, a staggering amount of 809,000 tonnes of food waste was produced in Singapore.(NEA) In a bit to alleviate this ongoing problem, surveys and interviews were conducted with consumers, businesses and governing authorities before we arrived on our solution of a digital marketplace that sells food surplus. Being accessible, convenient it is a triple win for consumers and businesses on costs, and environmentaly on the cause.

Macdonald's Japan

The Macdelivery Japan Mobile App was a client project undertaken during a Summer Internship at Visa's Design Studio in 2017. Designed according to human-centric needs, the app served as a solution to the inconvenience of food ordering and delivery in Japan.

Problem and Solution

Long complicated ordering processes are a no-no. By simplifying the user work flow with minimal clicks and steps, ordering turns into a easy and convenient process.

Outcome: User Intuivity

Along with an organised information architecture, button elements and use of font colour and weight helps a user proceed to the next step intuitvely.

Singapore Airlines App Challenge 2017

Along with 3 students from Singapore Manangement University (SMU), our team of 4 emerged as a Top 5 Finalist out of 40 teams in the student category. We tackled SIA's business challenge to build brand loyalty among youths by introducing gamification.

Focus on the user, and the rest will follow

Empathy - Being a youth myself, I started by questioning the things that draws my attention and keeps me hooked. How would I implement it on others when I am not drawn to it myself?

Visa Design Hackathon

I was involved in customising the 30-page app to the looks and feels for 31 banks across Singapore, Malaysia, Thailand, Myanmar, Cambodia, Indonesia and the Phillippines. This process was part of converting a hackathon design into a tangible app to be pushed into the market. Banks include DBS, Standard Chartered, Kasikornbank and BDO.

Process

It was important to note the primary and secondary colours of each brand to employ on buttons and other elements to maintain a professional and inspiring look and feel
